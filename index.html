It's been almost 20 years since the release of the library that in many ways popularised JavaScript and turned it into what it is today. A few years later, we started seeing frameworks pop up, and a new era of the frontend began. 7 years after the launch of jQuery, Facebook open sourced React.js, and its component based approach sent a large part of the existing frontend JavaScript frameworks and the web platform itself spinning. Eventually other frameworks like Svelte came along, alongside React meta frameworks like Next.js
